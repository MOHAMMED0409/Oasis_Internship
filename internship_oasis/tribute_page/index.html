Tribute to A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

Introduction

This tribute page is dedicated to A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, a renowned scientist and the 11th President of India. Known as the "Missile Man of India," Abdul Kalam played a crucial role in India's space and defense programs. He was not only a great scientist but also a source of inspiration for millions through his humble nature, dedication, and vision for a prosperous and technologically advanced India.

Early Life and Education

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India. He came from a humble background and overcame several obstacles to pursue his education. Abdul Kalam studied physics and aerospace engineering, earning degrees from St. Joseph's College and Madras Institute of Technology. He played a significant role in India's missile development programs, including the successful Pokhran-II nuclear tests.

"Dream, dream, dream. Dreams transform into thoughts and thoughts result in action." - A.P.J. Abdul Kalam
